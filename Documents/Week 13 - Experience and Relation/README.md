# Week 13 - Experience and Relation

## Core Question
**What kind of human experience or relation is the system staging?**

## Reading Assignment
Read the following before class:

1. Forlizzi, J., and Battarbee, K. (2004). *Understanding experience in interactive systems.*
2. Zhang, C.-B., Li, T.-G., Li, Y.-N., Chang, Y., and Zhang, Z.-P. (2024). *Fostering well-being: Exploring the influence of user-AI assistant relationship types on subjective well-being.*
3. Li, H., and Zhang, R. (2024). *Finding love in algorithms: Deciphering the emotional contexts of close encounters with AI chatbots.*

Course readings folder: [Google Drive](https://drive.google.com/drive/folders/1kCWXifyNegE5GBZXKlC-oh20U71H6HJi?usp=sharing)

## Reading Focus
This week asks you to think about your project not only as a tool, but as an encounter.
What kind of participation does it invite, what role does the AI play, and what emotional or relational dynamics might emerge around it?

## What To Look For
As you read, identify four things:

1. The relation being staged
- Is the AI operating as tool, tutor, partner, audience, critic, companion, or something else?

2. The shape of participation
- What does the experience ask the human to do, notice, feel, or become responsible for?

3. The affective consequence
- What forms of attachment, support, unease, dependence, or expectation might arise?

4. A project implication
- What kind of encounter is your project ultimately creating?

## Crucial Questions
Come to class ready to discuss:

1. What relation is being staged in this system?
2. What kind of participation is invited?
3. What does the experience ask the human to feel, notice, or do?
4. What social or ethical dynamics are being reproduced or challenged?
5. What kinds of attachment, support, or unease might emerge around your project?

## Weekly Structure
Each week in this part of the course, you do the same three things:

1. Complete the assigned readings.
2. Come prepared to discuss them.
3. Bring a short `Project Throughline` update.

## Project Throughline
Bring a short weekly update that responds to these prompts:

- `This week's question for my project is/was...`
- `One design decision I made because of it is/was...`
- `One thing I tested, sketched, or represented is/was...`
- `What changed in my project thinking is/was...`

This does not need to be code.
It can be a sketch, diagram, prompt flow, prototype, comparison, storyboard, or another concrete representation of your thinking.

## Possible Outcome
By the end of class, you should be able to describe your project as a designed human encounter, not only as a technical system or feature set.
