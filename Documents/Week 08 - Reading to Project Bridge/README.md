# Week 08 - Reading to Project Bridge

## Reading Focus
This week we use Shibata and Tanie to ask a practical design question:

**How does a designed system come to represent a human phenomenon, and what counts as evidence for that claim?**

The paper argues that social meaning does not live only inside a machine.
Meaning emerges through interaction, embodiment, prior knowledge, and interpretation.

## What to look for in the reading
As you read, identify four things:

1. The phenomenon claim
- What human phenomenon are the authors trying to understand or produce?

2. The representational move
- What did they build or stage in order to represent that phenomenon?
- Is the key representational move in the robot's form, behavior, interaction setup, or framing?

3. The evidence logic
- What kinds of evidence do the authors use?
- What do they treat as convincing?
- What remains weak, subjective, or under-argued?

4. The project lesson
- What is one move you could borrow, adapt, or reject for your own final project?

## In-class direction
Bring your Week 07 phenomenon statement if you have it.
Use the bridge below to write, discuss, or revise your thinking.

### Part 1 - My phenomenon
Write one sentence:

`When X happens in [context], Y changes.`

### Part 2 - One claim from the reading
Write 2-4 lines:
- What is one idea from Shibata and Tanie that matters for your project?
- Why does it matter?

### Part 3 - My representational move
Write 3-5 lines:
- What do you want to build: a test, statement, experience, interface, simulation, or artifact?
- What phenomenon will it represent or stage?
- What cues, forms, or interactions will matter most?

### Part 4 - My evidence plan
Write 3-5 lines:
- What evidence could support your claim?
- Observation notes?
- User interpretation?
- Comparative runs?
- Interaction logs?
- Screenshots, transcripts, or prototypes?

Be specific about what you will collect next.

## Possible output
By the end of class, students should ideally have some version of these four pieces, whether as notes, a verbal share-out, a discussion artifact, or a one-page draft:
1. Phenomenon statement
2. Reading claim
3. Representational move
4. Evidence plan

## Success criteria
- Your phenomenon is concrete and observable.
- You identify a real idea from the reading, not just a quote.
- Your project move is specific enough to prototype.
- Your evidence plan fits the kind of claim you are making.

## Helpful warning
Do not stop at "my system works."
If your project is making a claim about feeling, meaning, trust, attention, anthropomorphism, care, or interpretation, you need evidence about human sense-making, not only technical output.
