# Week 14 & 15 - Final Presentations

## Focus
Weeks 14 and 15 are final presentation days.

These sessions are dedicated to sharing, discussing, and reflecting on final projects developed over the semester.

See also:
- [Final Project Guide and Specification](./Final%20Project%20Guide%20and%20Specification.md)

## What To Prepare
Come ready to present your project clearly and concisely.

Your presentation should help the class understand:
- the phenomenon, question, or boundary your project addresses,
- the human-AI interaction, system, or experience you designed,
- the theoretical or conceptual ideas that shaped your decisions,
- what you built, tested, or represented,
- and what you learned from the process.

## Final Project Expectations
The final project should include:
- theoretical and conceptual analysis,
- a practical or computational artifact,
- and a reflective synthesis connecting theory, design decisions, and observed behavior.

The goal is not technical completeness.
The goal is epistemic clarity: showing how theory, design decisions, and computational behavior informed one another.

## Suggested Presentation Structure
You can organize your presentation in a simple sequence:

1. Project title and guiding question
2. Why this project matters
3. The concept or theory behind it
4. What you built
5. What happened when people interacted with it, or when you tested it
6. What changed in your thinking
7. What remains unresolved, limited, or open

## If You Are Sharing Materials
Bring whatever best helps make the project legible:
- slides,
- a live demo,
- screenshots or video,
- diagrams or process notes,
- or a short walkthrough of the artifact and its logic.
