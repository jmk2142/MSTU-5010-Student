# Week 09 - Agency and What Should Remain Human

## Core Question
**What should remain human in an AI-mediated experience?**

## Reading Assignment
Read the following before class:

1. Bandura, A. (2001). *Social cognitive theory: An agentic perspective.*
2. Darvin, R. (2025). *Identity and investment in the age of generative AI.*

Course readings folder: [Google Drive](https://drive.google.com/drive/folders/1kCWXifyNegE5GBZXKlC-oh20U71H6HJi?usp=sharing)

## Reading Focus
This week asks you to treat agency as a design question, not only a theory question.
As you read, pay attention to how each author helps you describe where action, judgment, responsibility, and decision-making are located in a human-AI system.

## What To Look For
As you read, identify four things:

1. A definition of agency
- What makes action agentic rather than automatic or mechanical?

2. A location of agency
- Is agency primarily located in a person, distributed across people and systems, or delegated through tools and platforms?

3. A design consequence
- If agency shifts, what changes about responsibility, authorship, interpretation, or revision?

4. A project implication
- What should the human in your project still interpret, judge, choose, or revise?

## Crucial Questions
Come to class ready to discuss:

1. What does Bandura think makes action agentic?
2. What changes when agency is distributed across sociotechnical systems?
3. What is gained by treating AI as proxy agency, and what gets obscured?
4. What do you want the human in your project to still interpret, judge, choose, or revise?
5. If your project leaves too little to the human, what is lost?

## Weekly Structure
Each week in this part of the course, you do the same three things:

1. Complete the assigned readings.
2. Come prepared to discuss them.
3. Bring a short `Project Throughline` update.

## Project Throughline
Bring a short weekly update that responds to these prompts:

- `This week's question for my project is/was...`
- `One design decision I made because of it is/was...`
- `One thing I tested, sketched, or represented is/was...`
- `What changed in my project thinking is/was...`

This does not need to be code.
It can be a sketch, diagram, prompt flow, prototype, comparison, storyboard, or another concrete representation of your thinking.

## Possible Outcome
By the end of class, you should ideally have a clearer position on:
- where agency is located in your project,
- what should remain human,
- and why that design choice matters.
