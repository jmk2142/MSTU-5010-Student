# Week 10
## AI as Mindtool

**Thinking, problem-solving, and cognitive support**

---

## Week 09 -> Week 10

Last week:
- What should remain human?

This week:
- What kind of thinking is worth designing for?
- When should AI help people think rather than think for them?

---

## Jonassen's Position

Technology should not mainly instruct.

Students should learn:
- **with** technology
- not only **from** technology

Computers can function as:
- mindtools
- knowledge construction tools
- supports for reasoning

---

## What is a mindtool?

A mindtool is a tool that requires the learner to:
- organize knowledge
- model relationships
- interpret information
- compare alternatives
- represent what they know

The point is not convenience.
The point is engagement in thinking.

---

## Jonassen's contrast

Bad framing:
- technology as tutor
- technology as delivery system
- technology as answer source

Better framing:
- technology as support for knowledge construction
- technology as a scaffold for reasoning

---

## Clark broadens the picture

Humans are already hybrid thinkers.

The question is not:
- does AI contaminate pure human thought?

The question is:
- when does AI extend thought?
- when does it replace it badly?
- what makes a good human-AI coalition?

---

## Vendrell & Johnston sharpen the design issue

Unstructured AI use can lead to:
- cognitive offloading
- metacognitive disengagement
- epistemic narrowing

Their answer:
- preserve cognitive friction
- use AI as a thinking partner
- embed evaluation
- balance AI-mediated and AI-free phases

---

## Useful synthesis

Jonassen:
- mindtools support thinking

Clark:
- AI may become part of extended thinking

Vendrell & Johnston:
- but only if we design for effort, evaluation, and agency

---

## Thinking worth designing for

Examples:
- comparison
- judgment
- problem framing
- interpretation
- explanation
- revision
- argumentation
- exploration of alternatives

Not every project needs the same kind of thinking.

---

## Key question

What kind of problem or cognitive engagement does your project invite?

Is it about:
- deciding?
- interpreting?
- generating options?
- critiquing?
- making sense of ambiguity?
- revising an idea?

---

## Important warning

If the system removes all struggle,
it may also remove the thinking you hoped to support.

Convenience is not the same as learning.
Fluency is not the same as understanding.
