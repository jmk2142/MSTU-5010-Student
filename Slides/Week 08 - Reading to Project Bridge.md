# Week 08
## Reading to Project Bridge

**Shibata and Tanie: representation, interpretation, evidence**

---

## Opening question

What is this paper actually trying to explain?

Not just:
- how a robot works

But:
- what kind of human meaning emerges in interaction
- how a designed form invites interpretation
- what counts as evidence for that claim

---

## Week 07 -> Week 08

Last week:
- name a phenomenon

- propose a representation direction

This week:
- test that direction against a reading

- revise your project idea accordingly

---

## Big claim from the reading

For human-facing robots, value is not only:
- speed
- accuracy
- efficiency

It is also:
- comfort
- interpretation
- subjective evaluation
- relational meaning

---

## Key distinction

### Objective performance is not the same as subjective interpretation

Questions:
- What did the robot do?
- What did the person think it meant?
- What prior knowledge shaped that interpretation?

---

## Why cat vs seal matters

The paper suggests:
- familiar forms create stronger expectation baselines
- unfamiliar forms may allow looser interpretation
- interaction is shaped by what people already know

So:
- design is never only the object
- design also includes expectation management

---

## Discussion lens 1

### Phenomenon claim

What human phenomenon are the authors studying?

Possibilities:
- comfort
- affective attachment
- interpretation of lifelike behavior
- social meaning through embodied contact

---

## Discussion lens 2

### Representational move

How do they represent the phenomenon?

Through:
- animal form
- embodied interaction
- staged encounters
- subjective evaluation instruments

---

## Discussion lens 3

### Evidence logic

What counts as evidence here?

- interviews
- reported comfort
- comparative responses
- differences across animal forms

What still feels thin or contestable?

---

## Salomon bridge

Where is this paper analytic?
- isolating design variables like form and prior familiarity

Where is it systemic?
- treating interaction as co-produced by body, context, memory, and interpretation

Both lenses are present.

---

## Project bridge

For your final project, define:

1. What phenomenon are you representing?
2. What form will stage it?
3. What kind of interpretation do you hope to invite?
4. What evidence would support that claim?

---

## Warning

Do not overclaim from output alone.

If your project concerns:
- emotion
- trust
- care
- anthropomorphism
- meaning

then your evidence must include human interpretation, not only system behavior.

---

## While reading Bandura, ask

- Where does he locate agency?
- What does he think makes human action agentic rather than mechanical?
- How useful is proxy agency for thinking about mediated systems or AI assistance?
- What does his model assume about the coherence of the human subject?

---

## While reading Darvin, ask

- How is agency distributed across humans and tools?
- How do platforms, datasets, and interfaces position users?
- What kinds of inequality shape who can act agentively?
- Why is critical digital literacy necessary, not optional?

---

## Comparative question

Which of these feels more accurate for the kinds of systems we have been studying?

1. AI as a new form of proxy agency
2. AI as a distributed sociotechnical system that reshapes agency itself

You do not need to choose one completely.
But you do need to decide what each explains better.
