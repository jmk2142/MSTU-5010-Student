## Why agency matters

Agency is not just a theory word.

It is a design question:
- What should the human still interpret?
- What should the human still judge?
- What should the human still choose?
- What should the human still revise?

---

## Bandura gives us one language

Key ideas:
- intentionality
- self-regulation
- reflection
- personal, proxy, and collective agency

Question:
- What makes action agentic rather than mechanical?

---

## While reading Bandura, ask

- Where does he locate agency?
- What makes human action agentic rather than mechanical?
- How useful is proxy agency for thinking about mediated systems or AI assistance?
- What does his model assume about the coherence of the human subject?

---

## Darvin gives us another language

Key ideas:
- distributed agency
- identity
- capital
- ideology
- sociotechnical systems

Question:
- How are people positioned by platforms, datasets, and interfaces?

---

## While reading Darvin, ask

- How is agency distributed across humans and tools?
- How do platforms, datasets, and interfaces position users?
- What kinds of inequality shape who can act agentively?
- Why is critical digital literacy necessary, not optional?

---

## Productive tension

Bandura asks:
- how humans exercise agency

Darvin asks:
- what changes when agency is entangled with AI systems and unequal infrastructures

We need both.

---

## Shared ground

Both readings reject simple determinism.

- People are shaped by systems, but not reducible to them.
- Agency is mediated, not purely isolated.
- Reflexivity matters in both accounts.

---

## Useful hinge

### Proxy agency

Bandura helps us ask:
- Am I acting through another system?
- What does it mean to rely on another actor with greater power, access, or expertise?

Darvin complicates that:
- What if the "other" is opaque?
- What if it also positions me?
- What if it reproduces ideology while helping me?

---

## Contrast

- Bandura centers agency in the human subject.
- Darvin distributes agency across people and sociotechnical systems.
- Bandura's main problem is reductionism.
- Darvin's main problem is infrastructural and ideological power.

---

## Complement

- Bandura helps explain why human agency still matters.
- Darvin helps explain why agency is never neutral or purely individual.
- Bandura gives a language for intentional action.
- Darvin gives a language for mediation, inequality, and platform conditions.
- Together, they help us talk about mediated agency in AI systems.

---

## Comparative prompt

1. Where does Bandura locate agency?
2. Where does Darvin locate agency?
3. What does each reading explain well?
4. What does each miss?
5. Which feels more accurate for the kinds of systems we have been studying?

## Examples of "what should remain human"

- interpretation
- judgment
- choice among alternatives
- ethical responsibility
- revision
- sense-making
- framing the question
- deciding what counts as success

---

## Important warning

If the AI does too much:
- the user may lose ownership
- the project may stop being about learning, inquiry, or expression
- the interaction may become impressive but thin

The question is not:
- Can AI do this?

The question is:
- Should the human still do some part of this, and why?
