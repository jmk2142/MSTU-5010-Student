# Week 07
## From Phenomenon -> Inquiry -> Representation

**Salomon as method tool, bridge to Week 08**

---

## Today: 3 core questions

1. What phenomenon do I care about?
2. How can I understand it analytically and systemically?
3. How have others represented it, and what might I build?

Goal: move from interest -> inquiry stance -> representation idea.

---

## Question 1 (Grounding)

### What is your phenomenon, exactly?

Write one sentence:

**"When X happens in [context], Y seems to change."**

Constraints:
- Use a specific context (classroom, studio, group, platform, workflow)
- Name an observable change
- Keep it concrete enough to investigate this semester

---

## Question 1 prompts

If stuck, ask:
- Who/what is interacting?
- What shifts over time?
- Where does this show up (talk, behavior, artifact, decision)?
- Why does this matter to you?

Deliverable: 1 phenomenon statement per person.

---

## Question 2 (Epistemic tool)

### "In order to understand this phenomenon..."

Complete both stems:

- **Analytically, I can look at it by...**
  - isolating a variable, relation, or mechanism
- **Systemically, I can look at it by...**
  - tracing interdependence, context, and pattern

Both are tools, not identities.

---

## Question 2 prompts

Analytic lens:
- What single factor could I isolate first?
- What might I miss if I isolate too much?

Systemic lens:
- What relationships co-produce this phenomenon?
- What might I miss if everything stays broad?

Deliverable: 2 short approaches to the same phenomenon.

---

## Question 3 (Literature -> Representation)

### How are others representing this phenomenon computationally?

Use literature to find how researchers/designers have:
- modeled it
- measured it
- staged it as an interaction/experience

Then ask:
- What representation strategy might I adapt or challenge?

---

## Question 3 prompts

Identify at least one source and extract:
- What phenomenon they claim to represent
- Their representational form (model, simulation, interface, robot, prompt/system, dataset)
- What evidence they use to justify the representation

Then propose your direction:
- A test, statement, or experience you could design

---

## When reading Shibata & Tanie, look for...

1. **Phenomenon claim**  
What human phenomenon are they trying to explain?

2. **Epistemic stance**  
Where are they analytic, and where are they systemic?

3. **Representational move**  
How do robot forms/interactions represent the phenomenon?

4. **Evidence logic**  
What counts as evidence for their claim, and what are its limits?
